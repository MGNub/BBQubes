How to solve a 3x3 rubik's cube using beginner's method (Layer by Layer)

Step 1 : Solve the White Cross

  • Start by picking a white edge piece and place it in the bottom layer so that the white sticker matches the center white sticker on the face.
  • Look for the corresponding center edge pieces and position them correctly around the white center, forming a white cross on the bottom face.

    • Step 2: Solve the White Corners

  • With the white cross already in place, locate the white corner pieces that belong to the bottom layer.
  • Align each corner piece with its respective position and orientation, ensuring the white sticker matches the bottom layer's color.

    • Step 3: Solve the Middle Layer

  • Focus on solving the middle layer by matching the edge pieces with the adjacent center pieces.
  • Find an edge piece with a color matching the center on the left or right side, and use the appropriate moves to place it in the middle layer.

    • Step 4: Solve the Yellow Cross (OLL - Orientation of the Last Layer)

  • Turn the Rubik's Cube upside down (with the solved layers at the bottom).
  • Aim to create a yellow cross on the top face by orienting the yellow edges correctly. There are different possible patterns you might encounter.

    • Step 5: Align the Yellow Edges (PLL - Permutation of the Last Layer)

  • Check for the arrangement of the yellow corners on the top layer. You will have one of four possible cases.
  • Use the corresponding algorithms to adjust the yellow edges' positions without disturbing the rest of the cube.

    • Step 6: Solve the Yellow Corners (PLL - Permutation of the Last Layer)

  • At this stage, focus on the orientation of the yellow corners on the top layer.
  • Depending on the orientation of the corners, apply the appropriate algorithm to correctly orient all of them.

    • Step 7: Finalize the Cube

  • Check the whole cube for any remaining incorrect positions or orientations of pieces.
  • Repeat the required algorithms to solve any last layer issues until you achieve a completely solved Rubik's Cube.
